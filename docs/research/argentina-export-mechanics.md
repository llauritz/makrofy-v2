# Argentina-side tax & FX mechanics for MoR app-subscription payouts

_Scope: pre-research for the accountant session (wayfinder ticket #62). Researched 2026-07-18 against ARCA/AFIP, BCRA, Boletín Oficial and Paddle/Polar primary sources. Everything here is to be verified by an Argentine accountant; confidence tags mark what is pinned vs open. Assumes: solo developer, tax-resident in Argentina, presumed monotributista, province unconfirmed; sells consumer app subscriptions worldwide via a Merchant of Record (Paddle UK or Polar US); MoR is the legal seller and remits aggregated USD payouts._

## TL;DR

- **Q1 — Factura E:** Monotributistas invoice service exports with Factura E via "Comprobantes en línea" (export point of sale required), in USD or ARS, CAE issued online; regime is [RG 4401/2019](https://www.sistemasagiles.com.ar/trac/wiki/FacturaElectronicaExportacion) amending RG 2758/3689 — **[firm]**. Invoicing the MoR (not end consumers), one Factura E per payout, is practitioner practice, not a written ARCA rule — **[likely]**. UK-vs-US counterparty: no Argentine-side difference; foreign-side withholding/forms (W-8BEN, royalty characterisation) — **[unverified]**.
- **Q2 — Monotributo caps:** Export receipts **do count** toward category caps — ARCA says so explicitly; the circulating "exports don't count" claim has no norm behind it (appears to be Mono-Tech-bill confusion) — **[firm]**. Current top cap (cat. K, from 1/02/2026): ARS 108,357,084.05/yr — **[firm]**, semi-annually indexed. What changes on outgrowing: Ganancias up to 35%, autónomos, monthly IVA filings (exports themselves stay IVA-free) — **[likely]**.
- **Q3 — IIBB:** Exported services are exempt in CABA (Código Fiscal, interpretation Res. AGIP 288/2010) and *not taxed* (excluded from base) in PBA, with an ARBA 0%-withholding padrón for pure exporters — **[likely]** (article numbers shift yearly; province-specific confirmation needed). Convenio Multilateral irrelevant for a single-province pure exporter — **[likely]**.
- **Q4 — FX:** Post-cepo (Com. "A" 8226, 14/04/2025) plus Com. "A" 8330 (18/09/2025, removes the USD 36,000/yr cap) and Com. "A" 8417 (09/04/2026, extends to all service concepts): a persona humana must still **bring export proceeds into Argentina within 20 hábiles days** (t.o. Exterior y Cambios pto. 2.2.1) but is **excepted from converting to pesos** — funds can sit in a local USD account, no amount limit — **[firm]**. Whether Payoneer/Stripe balances start/stop the 20-day clock — **[unverified]**.
- **Q5 — Mono-Tech:** Effectively dead. Media sanción in Diputados was 28/03/2023 (not 2026), Senate committee dictamen mid-2023, never voted, never enacted; no 2025–2026 reintroduction found — **[likely]**.
- **Q6 — AR consumers via MoR:** Consumer-side IVA 21% on foreign digital services (RG 4240/18) and the RG 5617/2024 30% percepción hit the *consumer/MoR sale*, not the developer — **[firm]**. Whether the developer's invoice to the MoR stays a clean export when end users are Argentine ("utilización efectiva" look-through) is genuinely unsettled — **[unverified]**; geo-blocking Argentina is a defensible simplification pending accountant sign-off.
- **Payout rails:** Paddle pays out worldwide (wire/SWIFT or Payoneer; only sanctioned countries excluded) — **[firm]**. Polar lists 🇦🇷 Argentina among Stripe Connect Express payout countries — **[firm]**; payout currency on arrival (USD vs forced ARS conversion) — **[unverified]**.

## Q1 — Factura E mechanics & cadence

**Regime.** Electronic export invoicing for services is governed by RG 2758/2010 as amended by [RG 4401/2019 (replacing RG 3689/2014)](https://www.sistemasagiles.com.ar/trac/wiki/FacturaElectronicaExportacion), covering "facturas de exportación clase E" plus class-E credit/debit notes. Monotributistas are expressly included: the official page [ARCA — Exportación de servicios (Monotributo)](https://www.afip.gob.ar/monotributo/exportacion-servicios/) says exports are invoiced with **comprobante clase "E"** issued through **"Comprobantes en línea"**, after enabling a dedicated **punto de venta for "Comprobantes de exportación"**. CAE is issued immediately online; up to 2,400 comprobantes/yr through the online service ([AFIP ABC — RG 2758/4401](https://servicioscf.afip.gob.ar/publico/abc/ABCpaso2.aspx?id_nivel1=562&id_nivel2=603&id_nivel3=3216&id_nivel4=3225&id_nivel5=3227&p=RG+2758+/+RG+4401+-+Operaciones+de+Exportaci%C3%B3n&f=1)). [firm]

**Currency.** Factura E can be issued in USD (and other enabled currencies) or in ARS. Foreign-currency invoices are converted at the **Banco Nación tipo de cambio of the business day prior to issuance** (sources vary between "comprador" and "vendedor divisa" wording — the AFIP ABC says *vendedor divisa*, the monotributo page says *comprador*; pin with the accountant which applies to services exports) ([AFIP ABC](https://servicioscf.afip.gob.ar/publico/abc/ABCpaso2.aspx?id_nivel1=562&id_nivel2=603&id_nivel3=3216&id_nivel4=3225&id_nivel5=3226); [ARCA monotributo page](https://www.afip.gob.ar/monotributo/exportacion-servicios/)). [firm on mechanics, open on which BNA rate]

**Foreign counterparty data.** The Comprobantes en línea flow asks for the foreign customer's name/razón social, country, address, and foreign tax ID if any; AFIP supplies **generic per-country CUITs ("CUIT del país", persona física vs jurídica)** selectable in the form, and the customer's condición frente al IVA is the export/"cliente del exterior" category ([tusfacturas.app guide](https://www.tusfacturas.app/factura-electronica-exportacion-servicios-monotributo.html); [Contablix 2026 guide](https://contablix.ar/blog/factura-e-exportacion-servicios-2026)). [likely — UI-level detail from secondary guides]

**Cadence.** No norm prescribes "one invoice per payout" for service exporters. General invoicing timing rules make the invoice due when the service period concludes or the price is collected, whichever first, and practitioner guidance aligns invoices with each cobro from abroad ([evemuriel — cómo facturar servicios al exterior](https://evemuriel.com/blog/monotributo/como-facturar-servicios-al-exterior.html)). For an MoR remitting monthly, the natural pattern practitioners describe is **one Factura E per monthly payout, to the MoR, for the net amount remitted** (fees already deducted) — but net-vs-gross invoicing (invoice gross sales and expense the MoR fee vs invoice the net payout) is exactly the kind of characterisation the accountant must settle. [likely / accountant]

**Invoicing the MoR.** Under a true MoR contract the developer's only counterparty is Paddle/Polar (the MoR resells to consumers in its own name — [Paddle model description](https://www.paddle.com/)), so the Factura E goes to the MoR's entity (Paddle.com Market Ltd, UK / Polar Software Inc, US), not to end consumers. Some generic blogs muddle this ("invoice the foreign client regardless of platform") because they discuss freelancing marketplaces, not MoRs. No AFIP pronouncement specifically on MoRs was found. [likely / accountant to confirm characterisation]

**UK vs US counterparty.** Argentine-side mechanics are identical (same Factura E, generic country CUIT). Differences live on the foreign side: Argentina has a DTA with the UK (Ley 24.727) and **no income-tax treaty with the US**; service fees generally attract no UK/US withholding, but if payouts were characterised as **royalties** a 30% US withholding could bite absent a treaty — and Polar/Stripe will ask for a W-8BEN. Not pinned to primary sources in this pass. [unverified — accountant + platform docs]

## Q2 — Monotributo caps

**Exports count.** The official ARCA position: *"Los monotributistas pueden exportar servicios, sin superar los límites de facturación de la categoría máxima correspondiente a su actividad"* ([ARCA — Exportación de servicios](https://www.afip.gob.ar/monotributo/exportacion-servicios/)). Ingresos brutos for categorisation are the accrued (devengados) receipts of the activity — there is no carve-out for exports in the Anexo of Ley 24.977 or its reglamentation that this research could find. [firm]

**The circulating "don't count" claim.** Blogs assert Factura E income "no suma al tope" (e.g. [yo-facturo](https://yo-facturo.com/blog/como-facturar-al-exterior-monotributo/)); no norm supports it. The most plausible origin is the **Mono-Tech bill** (which would have created a separate USD-denominated regime — see Q5) and/or confusion with the *FX* exemption (USD 12k/24k/36k, an exchange-control rule, never a tax-cap rule). Treat the claim as false unless the accountant can point to a norm. [likely-false claim / accountant to confirm no carve-out]

**Current caps.** Values effective **1/02/2026** ([ARCA — categorías](https://www.afip.gob.ar/monotributo/categorias.asp)): A $10,277,988.13 … H $70,113,407.33 … K $108,357,084.05 annual ingresos brutos, same caps for services and goods since the Ley 27.743 reform. Caps are indexed semi-annually by IPC (next update applies from August 2026 alongside the July recategorización — [iProfesional](https://www.iprofesional.com/impuestos/445156-arca-ex-afip-confirmo-valores-del-monotributo-que-rigen-desde-enero-de-2026)). At ~US$4–12/month and low volume, headroom is enormous (cat. K ≈ US$85–90k/yr at mid-2026 FX). [firm]

**Outgrowing → Responsable Inscripto.** Exports of services remain outside IVA either way (see Q6 — Ley de IVA art. 1°, services rendered in-country but "utilizados o explotados efectivamente en el exterior" are not taxed; as RI you can even recover input IVA credits tied to exports). What actually changes: **Impuesto a las Ganancias** on net income at the progressive scale (top 35%) instead of the flat monotributo quota, **autónomos** social-security contributions, monthly IVA filings (mostly zero-activity for a pure exporter), and bookkeeping/admin. [likely — standard practitioner account, e.g. [iProfesional on export tax questions](https://www.iprofesional.com/impuestos/370351-exportacion-de-servicio-que-cuestiones-tributarias-contemplar)]

## Q3 — Ingresos Brutos (provincial)

- **CABA:** export of services is **exempt** — Res. [AGIP 288/2010](https://archivo.consejo.org.ar/noticias18/files/Acta_Grupo_de_Enlace_AGIP-180418.pdf) interprets the Código Fiscal exemption as covering "prestaciones o servicios efectuados en el país cuya utilización o explotación se lleve a cabo en el exterior"; practitioner references place it at art. 142 inc. 22 of the Código Fiscal (numbering shifts with each year's reordering — verify current article). Exempt income is still declared (loaded as exempt activity in the monthly ISIB DDJJ / e-Sicol) ([evemuriel — IIBB y exportaciones](https://evemuriel.com/blog/ingresos-brutos/exportaciones-exentas-ingresos-brutos.html)). [likely]
- **Provincia de Buenos Aires:** exports of goods/services to third countries are **excluded from the tax base ("no gravado")** — Código Fiscal PBA art. 186 inc. c) per practitioner references — and ARBA offers a specific trámite so taxpayers with **"Actividad exclusiva de exportación de servicios"** enter the recaudación padrones at **0%** ([ARBA guía de trámites](https://www.arba.gov.ar/GuiaTramites/TramiteSeleccionado.asp?categ=34&tramite=666&mostrar=S); [Consejo/Trivia note on the 0% benefit](https://trivia.consejo.org.ar/ficha/526402-buenos_aires._beneficio_del_0_de_ingresos_brutos_para_exportadores_de_servicios)). "No gravado" (PBA) vs "exento" (CABA) matters procedurally but both end at zero IIBB on export income. [likely]
- **Variation & Convenio Multilateral:** treatment does vary by province (most exempt or exclude exported services, but registration/percepción-padrón hygiene differs), so the developer's actual province must be confirmed. Convenio Multilateral only applies when the activity spans multiple Argentine jurisdictions; a pure exporter working from one province is a local (jurisdiction-only) IIBB taxpayer — CM registration not needed. Watch out for **bank percepciones (SIRCREB/SIRCUPA-style)** on account credits if not enrolled as exempt/0% — a classic leak for exporters. [likely / accountant]

## Q4 — Landing USD payouts (post-2025 FX regime)

**The 2025 dismantling.** [Com. "A" 8226 (11/04/2025)](https://aduananews.com/actualidad-normativa-cambiaria-comunicacion-a-8226/) ended the cepo for personas humanas from 14/04/2025: no more USD 200/month cap or restriction cross-checks for buying FX via bank accounts (cash purchases capped at USD 100/month), dólar blend eliminated, band-floating FX ([contadoresenred summary](https://contadoresenred.com/comunicacion-a-8226-bcra/); [Chequeado explainer](https://chequeado.com/el-explicador/fin-del-cepo-cambiario-cuantos-dolares-se-pueden-comprar-en-efectivo-y-cuantos-por-home-banking-a-partir-del-lunes/)). [firm]

**Current rule for a persona humana exporting services** (texto ordenado ["Exterior y Cambios"](https://www.bcra.gob.ar/archivos/Pdfs/Texord/t-excbio.pdf), Sección 2):

1. **Ingreso obligation stands:** pto. 2.2.1 — *"Los cobros por la prestación de servicios por parte de residentes a no residentes deberán ser ingresados y liquidados en el mercado de cambios en un plazo no mayor a los 20 (veinte) días hábiles a partir de la fecha de su percepción en el exterior o en el país o de su acreditación en cuentas del exterior."* [firm — verbatim from the t.o.]
2. **…but liquidation (peso conversion) is excepted for personas humanas:** pto. 2.2.2.1 excepts service-export cobros where funds are **credited to a foreign-currency account of the client at a local financial entity** (condition ii), with a **declaración jurada** and the condition that the mechanism be **"neutra en materia fiscal"** (conditions iv–v). [firm — verbatim]
3. **The old personal caps are gone:** the USD 12,000/yr exception (Com. "A" 7518, 02/06/2022, later 24k then 36k) was capped at **USD 36,000/yr** until [Com. "A" 8330 (18/09/2025)](https://www.bcra.gob.ar/archivos/Pdfs/comytexord/A8330.pdf) — *"Dejar sin efecto el límite anual de USD 36.000 … habilitando para las personas humanas el mecanismo de excepción de la obligación de liquidación … sin límite de monto"* — which also bans bank commissions on crediting those inflows ([Boletín Oficial 23/09/2025](https://www.boletinoficial.gob.ar/detalleAviso/primera/331822/20250923)). [firm]
4. **All service concepts now qualify:** [Com. "A" 8417 (09/04/2026)](https://www.bcra.gob.ar/archivos/Pdfs/comytexord/A8417.pdf) pto. 2 — *"Extender a todos los conceptos de servicios la excepción a la obligación de liquidación de cobros de exportaciones de servicios que realicen personas humanas."* (Our case was already covered as **S13 Servicios de informática** / arguably **S15 propiedad intelectual** in pto. 2.2.2.1.i.) The same communication extended the exception to goods exports by individuals (pto. 1) ([Boletín Oficial 13/04/2026](https://www.boletinoficial.gob.ar/detalleAviso/primera/340665/20260413); [Allende & Brea note](https://allende.com/bancario/el-banco-central-flexibiliza-el-regimen-cambiario-para-exportaciones-transferencias-en-moneda-extranjera-y-pagos-financieros-04-14-2026/)). [firm]

**Net effect:** each MoR payout should be wired into an **Argentine cuenta en dólares within 20 hábiles days of receipt abroad**; it can then stay in USD indefinitely (or be sold at the free FX rate) — no forced peso conversion, no amount cap ([smartlog summary](https://www.smartlog.com.ar/blog/exportacion-de-servicios-abril-2026-nueva-opcion-para-no-liquidar-divisas/)). Miss the 20-day window and the exception is lost — the cobro must then be liquidated to pesos ([aduananews on A 8417](https://aduananews.com/com-a-8417-resumen-de-los-principales-cambios-en-el-regimen-cambiario/)). [firm on rule; enforcement practice for small individual exporters — accountant]

**Intermediaries (Payoneer / Stripe balances).** The 20-day clock runs from "percepción … o acreditación en cuentas del exterior". Whether a Payoneer balance or a Stripe Connect pending balance is a "cuenta del exterior" acreditación (starting the clock) — and whether routing Paddle→Payoneer→AR bank keeps you compliant — is a known practitioner grey zone; pre-2025 guidance treated Payoneer as valid provided funds ultimately entered within plazo. Not pinned to a BCRA text naming these rails. [unverified — accountant]

**How each MoR lands, practically:**
- **Paddle:** payouts via **wire (SWIFT)** or **Payoneer**; Paddle "can payout to anywhere in the world with exception to sanctioned countries"; currency-mismatched wires carry a ~US$15 fee ([Paddle Help — when and how do I get paid](https://www.paddle.com/help/manage/get-paid/when-and-how-do-i-get-paid); [payout fees](https://www.paddle.com/help/manage/get-paid/is-there-a-fee-taken-for-payouts)). A USD SWIFT wire into a local USD account maps cleanly onto the pto. 2.2.2.1 exception. [firm on Paddle docs]
- **Polar:** payouts via **Stripe Connect Express**, and **Argentina is on Polar's supported-countries list** ([Polar docs — supported countries](https://polar.sh/docs/merchant-of-record/supported-countries); [Polar on X confirming the Stripe Connect Express dependency](https://x.com/polar_sh/status/1915379610809782428)). Stripe's own cross-border payout docs gate some countries; whether the AR payout arrives in USD (usable for the USD-account exception) or is force-converted to ARS by Stripe's rails is not stated in the fetched docs. [firm that AR is supported; payout currency unverified]

## Q5 — Mono-Tech

The "Mono-Tech" (Régimen Simplificado y Cambiario para Pequeños Contribuyentes Tecnológicos) got **media sanción in Diputados on 28/03/2023** ([Palabras del Derecho](https://www.palabrasdelderecho.com.ar/articulo/4193/Monotributo-techs-Diputados-aprobo-el-Regimen-Simplificado-y-Cambiario-para-Pequenos-Contribuyentes-Tecnologicos); [El Economista](https://eleconomista.com.ar/politica/monotech-freelancers-diputados-aprobo-envio-senado-proyecto-beneficios-monotributistas-tecnologicos-n60986)) and a **Senate Presupuesto y Hacienda dictamen** in mid-2023 ([Senado prensa](https://www.senado.gob.ar/prensa/21038/noticias); [Ámbito](https://www.ambito.com/economia/monotributo-tech-el-proyecto-obtuvo-dictamen-el-senado-n5735877)), but was **never voted in the Senate and never enacted** — as of 2026 "no existe un régimen Monotributo Tech implementado" ([Lannis — qué pasó con el Monotributo Tech](https://lannis.app/blog/que-paso-con-el-monotributo-tech)). It would have created USD-denominated categories (US$10k/20k/30k per year), collection in dollars without liquidation, and a small unified tax. No 2025–2026 reintroduction surfaced; its raison d'être largely evaporated with the 2025 FX liberalisation (Q4). Track, don't plan on it. [likely — caducidad not verified against HCDN records]

## Q6 — Argentine end-consumers via the MoR

**Consumer-side (MoR's problem, not the developer's).** Sales by a non-resident of digital services to Argentine consumers are hit by **21% IVA on digital services** (Ley 27.430 amendment to the Ley de IVA; collection regime **[RG 4240/2018](https://www.afip.gob.ar/iva/servicios-digitales/reg-percepcion-4240.asp)**, percepción by the card issuer/payment intermediary when the provider is on the [Anexo II listado](https://www.afip.gob.ar/iva/servicios-digitales/prestadores.asp), updated periodically). The **impuesto PAIS lapsed in Dec 2024**, but **[RG 5617/2024](https://www.bancopatagonia.com.ar/preguntas-frecuentes/tarjetas/credito/percepcion.php)** keeps a **30% percepción a cuenta de Ganancias/Bienes Personales** on foreign-currency card consumption, still in force in 2026 ([iProfesional](https://www.iprofesional.com/impuestos/445594-dolar-tarjeta-paso-a-paso-para-no-pagar-la-percepcion-del-30-por-ciento-de-arca-ex-afip); [Argentina.gob.ar on the earlier eliminations](https://www.argentina.gob.ar/noticias/ganancias-y-bienes-personales-se-eliminaron-las-percepciones-cuenta-por-compras-de-moneda)). So an AR subscriber paying Paddle US$5 sees IVA (+ possibly the 30% percepción + provincial IIBB percepciones) added by their card issuer. None of this is remitted by or charged to the developer; whether Paddle/Polar are on the current RG 4240 listado (affects consumer pricing/friction) was not confirmed — check the [current listado file](https://www.afip.gob.ar/iva/servicios-digitales/prestadores.asp). [firm on regimes; Paddle/Polar listing unverified]

**Developer-side characterisation.** Export-of-services status turns on **"utilización o explotación efectiva" in the exterior** — "la utilización inmediata o el primer acto de disposición por parte del prestatario" ([ARCA — qué es exportación de servicios](https://www.afip.gob.ar/derechos-de-exportacion-de-servicios/que-es/); doctrine overview: [Marval](https://www.marval.com/publicacion/el-tratamiento-de-la-prestacion-de-servicios-internacionales-en-el-impuesto-al-valor-agregado-12957?lang=en)). The pro-export reading: the developer licenses/provides the software service **to the MoR**, whose first act of disposition (reselling worldwide in its own name) happens abroad — analogous to AFIP dictámenes accepting export status for services to foreign principals even when the underlying activity touches Argentina (e.g. Dictamen DAT 65/07 line of cases via [Consejo/Trivia](https://trivia.consejo.org.ar/ficha/18130-dictamen_dat_6507._exenciones._exportacion_de_servicios._utilizacion_economica._asesoramiento_en_compras_a_empresas_del_exterior._servicios_de_informacion_sobre_el_mercado_argentino_que_brinda_una_emp)). The risk reading: for the slice of subscribers located in Argentina, ARCA could argue utilización efectiva occurs in Argentina and re-characterise that slice as a local supply (for a monotributista this mostly threatens the Factura E/FX-concept treatment and IIBB exemption rather than creating IVA, since monotributo subsumes IVA — but it becomes material as Responsable Inscripto). No AFIP pronouncement specifically on the MoR structure was found. [unverified — this is the core accountant question]

**Geo-blocking.** Given low launch volume, tiny expected AR revenue, consumer-side surcharges (~21% IVA + 30% percepción making the product expensive locally), and the characterisation risk above, geo-blocking Argentina at launch looks like a **defensible, low-cost simplification** — it removes the utilización-efectiva ambiguity entirely. Confirm with the accountant that nothing else (e.g. CV of the app being usable while traveling) undermines it. [likely]
